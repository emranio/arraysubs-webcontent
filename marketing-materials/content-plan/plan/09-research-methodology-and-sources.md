# Research methodology and source register

## Inputs

- Local product source: `information.md`, live Next.js pages/data, sitemap, feature inventory, use-case inventory, 127 recipe records, seven comparison pages, and the 2026 local research files.
- Live competitor/vendor research on 2026-07-10: WooCommerce, WP Swings, WPSubscription, WebToffee, Sublium/FunnelKit, SureCart, YITH, SUMO, RetentionStack, RetainFlow, MemberPress, Paid Memberships Pro, WPBeginner, Kinsta, and other ranking publishers.
- Current platform guidance: Google Search Central, Bing Webmaster, and OpenAI publisher documentation.
- Current search-result samples for subscription plugins, memberships, recurring payments, churn/recovery, gateways, checkout, store credit, analytics, boxes, comparisons, and vertical use cases.

## Confidence labels

- **Verified fact:** observed in current local code or a live primary source on the research date.
- **Observed pattern:** repeated across current SERPs or competitor content; useful strategically but not a traffic metric.
- **Modeled estimate:** inherited volume/KD or opportunity values from the local report; validate before forecasting.
- **Hypothesis:** an editorial or differentiation bet to test through impressions, rankings, citations, and conversion data.

## Main limitations

- No Google Search Console, analytics, paid keyword database, backlink index, or dedicated AI-monitor data was available.
- Search results vary by location, device, history, and date.
- Competitor prices, versions, plans, and feature tiers can change quickly.
- The plan intentionally avoids precise traffic forecasts until first-party data becomes available.

## Mandatory revalidation before drafting

Re-run the target SERP, inspect the top 10 formats, verify the primary focus keyword against Search Console/SEO tools if available, confirm no new ArraySubs page owns the intent, refresh competitor facts, and identify the original evidence the article will contribute.
