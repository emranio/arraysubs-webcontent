# Executive content strategy

## Outcome

Build the missing editorial authority layer around ArraySubs while protecting the site's strong bottom-funnel recipe, use-case, and comparison inventory. The plan balances traditional organic search with AI-answer visibility through original, well-sourced, extractable content rather than keyword repetition or special “AI schema.”

## Strategic rules

1. **One intent, one canonical page.** Existing commercial pages own product, pricing, feature, use-case, and ArraySubs-vs-competitor head terms. Editorial pages own education, strategy, diagnostics, neutral comparisons, and research.
2. **Answer first, prove next.** Give the useful answer inside the first 150 words, then support it with sourced facts, tests, screenshots, examples, tables, formulas, and limitations.
3. **Publish clusters, not isolated posts.** Release each editorial pillar with 3-5 supporting pieces and complete the internal links before opening another large cluster.
4. **Prefer non-commodity evidence.** ArraySubs should publish lab tests, current UI screenshots, reproducible troubleshooting, calculators, anonymized benchmarks, and transparent comparison methods.
5. **Do not overclaim.** Retention, analytics, and self-service now have several competitors and Woo add-ons. Position ArraySubs around integrated native-Woo breadth without assembling a fragmented stack.
6. **Treat word count as scope, not a ranking factor.** Each estimate is an editorial budget. Stop when the searcher's task is fully resolved.

## Success measures

- Search: non-brand impressions, top-20 and top-10 keyword count, organic conversions, assisted pricing visits, and cluster-level traffic.
- AI: cited URLs and grounding queries in Bing AI Performance, ChatGPT referral sessions (`utm_source=chatgpt.com`), manual citation audits, and assisted conversions.
- Quality: indexed pages, links earned, update SLA, expert-review completion, content decay, and cannibalization incidents.
- Business: free-to-pricing journeys, “View Pro Pricing” clicks, “Choose a Plan” clicks, trial/purchase conversions, and qualified support or migration inquiries.

## Recommended URL architecture

Use `/articles/<category>/<evergreen-slug>/`. Keep years out of URLs even when a title uses “2026,” and maintain category hubs plus a unified resources hub, RSS feed, author pages, editorial policy, and real `dateModified` values.
