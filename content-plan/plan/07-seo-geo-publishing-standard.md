# SEO and GEO publishing standard

## What current platform guidance actually says

- Google says its normal crawl, index, quality, page-experience, internal-link, and people-first guidance applies to AI Overviews and AI Mode; no special AI markup is required.
- Bing recommends clear headings, tables, FAQs where useful, evidence, freshness, and unambiguous entity consistency; Bing Webmaster Tools now exposes AI citations and grounding queries.
- OpenAI says public pages can appear in ChatGPT search when OAI-SearchBot can crawl them. This site's current wildcard robots rule allows it.
- FAQ/HowTo structure can help readers and answer extraction, but do not promise Google FAQ or HowTo rich results. Structured data must match visible content and supported current features.

Primary guidance: [Google AI features](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/ai-features), [Google people-first content](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content), [Bing AI Performance](https://blogs.bing.com/webmaster/February-2026/Introducing-AI-Performance-in-Bing-Webmaster-Tools-Public-Preview), [OpenAI publisher FAQ](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/12627856-publishers-and-developers-faq).

## Required article anatomy

1. Descriptive H1 and a 40-60 word direct answer within the first 150 words.
2. Key takeaways with 3-5 specific conclusions.
3. Clear definitions on first use with full entity/product names.
4. A question-matched structure: process, comparison, formula, decision tree, or diagnostic path.
5. At least one extractable original table/checklist/formula/diagram.
6. Primary-source citations placed beside claims; no source dumping at the end.
7. Original screenshots/test results when software behavior is discussed.
8. Limitations, edge cases, and “not a fit when” guidance.
9. Named author and technical reviewer bios.
10. Published and last-verified dates plus a meaningful update log.
11. Contextual internal links to one commercial pillar, one recipe/manual page, and 2-3 siblings.
12. CTA only after the question is answered.

## Comparison standard

Record test date, version, price currency/region, environment, criteria, evidence URLs, affiliate/commercial disclosure, and update trigger. Include best-for/not-for, limitations, TCO, migration risk, data ownership, gateway coverage, and a neutral verdict by use case. Never claim a competitor lacks a feature without current primary-source verification.

## Data and benchmark standard

Publish methodology, sample size, date range, definitions, exclusions, formulas, uncertainty, and downloadable anonymized data where safe. Do not invent churn rates, recovery rates, customer results, quotes, installs, or ROI. If original data is not available, rename the piece as a sourced guide instead of a “benchmark report.”

## Product truth gate

Verify ArraySubs feature tier/status from current code/product pages. Never market coming-soon features as shipped. For legal, tax, SCA, negative-option, privacy, accounting, or compliance content, obtain qualified review and state that the article is general information, not professional advice.
