## Data Safety

**Last Reviewed:** April 10, 2026

This page provides a plain-language summary of data safety for two distinct contexts: visitors to this website (arraysubs.com), and merchants who use the ArraySubs plugin on their own WooCommerce store.

---

## This Website (arraysubs.com)

### What We Collect

| Data | Collected? | Details |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Google Analytics (page views, sessions) | ✅ Yes | GA4 with IP anonymisation |
| Advertising / retargeting pixels | ❌ No | Not used |
| Heatmaps / session recordings | ❌ No | Not used |
| Email marketing lists | ❌ No | No newsletter or marketing automation |
| Social media tracking pixels | ❌ No | Not used |
| Contact form submissions | ✅ Yes | Name, email, message — used only to reply |

### How We Protect It

- All traffic served over HTTPS (TLS 1.2+)
- No sensitive data stored in browser local storage or cookies beyond GA4 identifiers and consent preference
- Contact form data deleted within 12 months of resolution
- Access to personal data (contact form submissions) limited to the data controller (ArrayHash)

### Analytics Consent

Visitors in the EU/EEA, UK, and other jurisdictions where analytics consent is legally required are shown a consent banner before the GA4 script loads. The consent preference is stored in the `cc_cookie` cookie and respected on all subsequent visits. You can withdraw consent at any time via the banner or by emailing emran@arrayhash.com.

## The ArraySubs Plugin (Your Store)

### Important Distinction

ArraySubs runs entirely within **your own WordPress/WooCommerce installation**. ArrayHash does not:

- Receive customer data from your store
- Connect to ArrayHash servers for telemetry or tracking
- Store any of your customers' personally identifiable information (PII)

Subscription and membership data is processed **locally on your server**.

### Data the Plugin Handles (on Your Server)

| Data Type | Stored Where | Controlled By |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Customer names, emails | Your WordPress database | You (the merchant) |
| Subscription status, billing dates | Your WordPress database | You (the merchant) |
| Payment tokens / method identifiers | Your payment gateway | Your payment gateway |
| Membership access records | Your WordPress database | You (the merchant) |

### Merchant Responsibilities Under GDPR

If you serve EU or UK customers using ArraySubs, **you are the Data Controller**. You are responsible for:

- Maintaining a privacy policy on your store
- Obtaining lawful consent for data collection at checkout
- Responding to data subject access and erasure requests from your customers
- Ensuring your payment gateway and hosting provider comply with applicable law

ArrayHash is not a data processor for your store's customer data.

## Data Breach Response

In the event of a personal data breach, ArrayHash will:

1. Contain and assess the breach within 24 hours of discovery
2. Notify affected individuals within 72 hours where required by GDPR
3. Report to the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours if the breach poses a risk to rights and freedoms
